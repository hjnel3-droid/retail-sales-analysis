## Overview

A comprehensive analysis of over 1 million retail transactions from a UK-based online retailer covering December 2009 to December 2011. This project explores product performance, seasonal trends, customer markets, and operational patterns to uncover actionable business insights.

---

## Key Findings

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| Top market | UK dominates revenue, with Ireland and Netherlands completing the top 3 |
| Revenue peak | November each year, driven by early Christmas and Black Friday purchasing |
| December decline | Sharp drop suggesting customer concerns around delivery lead times |
| Top product | Regency Cakestand 3 Tier — leads both revenue (£314,045) and units sold |
| Core trading window | 10:00–16:00 with a lunch hour peak at 12:00 |
| Cancellations | 19,104 recorded — two products show abnormally high volumes requiring investigation |
| Overall AOV | £382.05 — Netherlands, Australia and Thailand represent high AOV growth opportunities |

## Notebook Structure

The analysis is structured as a single Jupyter Notebook with the following sections:

**1. Data Loading & Exploration**
- Shape, column types, null value check, summary statistics

**2. Data Cleaning**
- Handling nulls, cancellations, and invalid records

**3. Revenue Analysis**
- Revenue by country
- Revenue by month
- Month-over-month growth rate
- International markets (excluding UK)

**4. Product Analysis**
- Top 10 products by revenue
- Top 10 products by quantity
- Worst performing products

**5. Operational Patterns**
- Revenue by day of week
- Revenue by hour of day

**6. Customer & Order Analysis**
- Average order value by country
- Cancellation analysis
